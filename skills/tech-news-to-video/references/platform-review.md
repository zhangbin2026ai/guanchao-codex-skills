# Platform Review

Use this checklist before delivering content for Douyin, Xiaohongshu, or WeChat Channels.

## Risk Checks

- **Facts**: Every major claim should have a source link, date, or clear “inference” label.
- **Speculation**: Replace certainty language with “可能”, “更像是”, “一个信号是” when evidence is incomplete.
- **Public affairs**: Flag party, government, military, national leaders, war, sanctions, sovereignty, ethnic, religious, and public-security topics.
- **Enterprise claims**: Avoid unsupported accusations against named companies or executives.
- **Investment claims**: Avoid “必涨”, “抄底”, “稳赚”, or direct financial advice.
- **Medical/legal claims**: Avoid professional advice unless sourced and caveated.
- **Copyright**: Do not copy article text, video scripts, creator wording, or watermarked footage.
- **Platform retention**: The first 30 seconds must contain conflict, stakes, and a reason to keep listening.

## Sensitive Framing Guidance

The goal is to reduce risk and improve clarity, not to bypass moderation.

- Prefer broad, neutral descriptions when direct naming creates unnecessary risk: “最高层”, “监管层”, “某大国”, “产业政策”, “军工体系”, “出口管制”.
- Use culturally common nicknames only when they are truthful, contextually clear, and not misleading. Do not use coded language to smuggle claims that would otherwise be unsafe.
- For party/government/military content, keep wording analytical and sourced. Avoid jokes about specific current officials, incitement, conspiracy certainty, or calls to action.
- If the core story depends on high-risk politics, recommend reframing toward industry chain, technology standards, supply constraints, or business consequences.

## Platform Title Notes

- **Douyin**: stronger conflict and curiosity, but avoid hard political bait.
- **Xiaohongshu**: ordinary-person perspective, “看懂趋势”, “别只看热闹”.
- **WeChat Channels**: steadier knowledge tone, more credible and less sensational.

## Review Output

Return:

- `审核结论`: publishable / revise before publishing / high risk.
- `主要风险`: short bullets.
- `建议改法`: concrete rewrites for risky claims, titles, or hooks.
- `留存判断`: whether the first 30 seconds creates enough reason to stay.
