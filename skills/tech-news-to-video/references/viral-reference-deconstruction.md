# Viral Reference Deconstruction

Use this reference when the user provides high-performing creator videos or asks to improve script expression.

Do not copy exact wording, punchlines, personal catchphrases, or paragraph order from a creator. Extract transferable narrative mechanics only.

## What To Extract

For each reference video, identify:

- title engine: what shock, number, named person, event, or consequence makes users stop
- first sentence: what does it make the viewer feel in three seconds
- audience bridge: how it translates hard terms into ordinary fear, money, job, phone, car, computer, or company scenes
- escalation ladder: what layer it opens next instead of staying on one theme
- conflict object: what concrete object carries the conflict, such as a PC, chip, screen, model, bank account, contract, data center, car camera, or power cable
- emotional turn: where the story moves from curiosity to danger, opportunity, anxiety, or urgency
- final takeaway: what sentence changes the meaning of the original news

## Common Mechanics In AI Viral Videos

High-retention AI videos often do these things:

- They do not begin with background. They begin with a disturbance.
- They avoid neutral product language. A product becomes a weapon, door, toll gate, replacement machine, or new table.
- They compress the public event into a short line, then quickly ask what it really changes.
- They connect one news item to a larger board: labor, finance, security, geopolitics, supply chain, or consumer hardware.
- They translate technical progress into a human consequence: someone loses time, money, choice, status, safety, or bargaining power.
- They make the viewer feel late to the story, but still able to catch up.

## Reference Pattern Notes

### Redwoods / Sequoia AI Ascent / Intelligence Aluminum

Useful mechanism:

- turn an abstract capital conference into a simple historical analogy
- compare intelligence to aluminum: once rare and expensive, then industrialized and everywhere
- the hidden story is not AGI as a slogan, but intelligence changing from scarce talent into cheap production input
- connect to labor pricing, startup moats, service automation, capital repricing, and who can still be scarce when intelligence becomes abundant

Story question:

> If intelligence is no longer rare, what becomes rare next?

### OpenAI Agent / AI Works Without Human Supervision

Useful mechanism:

- turn product update into loss of human supervision
- focus on time: AI can work while people sleep, cross screens, operate remotely, and keep running
- the hidden story is not tool improvement, but the transfer of execution power from humans to agents
- connect to work boundaries, software labor, enterprise trust, security risk, and who manages autonomous workers

Story question:

> When AI stops waiting for a human to click next, who is still the boss?

### NVIDIA N1X / New PC Era

Useful mechanism:

- turn chip rumor into an old throne being challenged
- PC is not only a computer; it is the worker's desk, developer's factory, and local AI terminal
- the hidden story is Nvidia moving from selling shovels to entering the desk itself
- connect to Microsoft, Arm, MediaTek, TSMC, Apple M-series, Qualcomm, local AI, and the future of AI PCs

Story question:

> If the PC becomes a small AI workstation, who owns the entrance to daily work?

## Rewrite Discipline

When improving a weak “观潮” draft:

1. Keep only the strongest disturbance.
2. Remove generic claims.
3. Replace abstract language with an object the viewer can see.
4. Add one adjacent news item only if it reveals a shared rule.
5. Move from event to consequence, not from concept to concept.
6. End with a question or sentence that makes the viewer re-label the original news.

Avoid:

- copying the creator's hype wording
- using too many exclamation phrases
- stacking numbers without meaning
- calling everything “炸圈,” “大招,” or “彻底变天”
- turning every topic into collapse anxiety

Use the energy, not the costume.
