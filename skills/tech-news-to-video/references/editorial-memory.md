## 2026-05-30 Topic Depth Revision

The user confirmed that the topic directions are broadly right, but the analysis must go deeper than surface news.

### Core Editorial Correction

Do not stop at:

- product release
- company action
- market heat
- abstract power migration

Push each topic into second-order consequences:

- which industry chain shifts
- which country or region may be replaced
- which old advantage decays
- which new advantage rises
- which system is trying to solve a bottleneck differently
- what debate this creates for viewers

### Apple / Smart Glasses / Asia Supply Chain

The Apple glasses angle should not only be “Apple occupies human vision.”

Deeper line:

- Apple may want to control the visual entrance through an American platform stack.
- But the device body may still depend on Asian manufacturing, optics, displays, chips, assembly, and precision components.
- The interesting story is the contradiction between platform control and supply-chain dependence.

Required follow-up questions:

- Is Japan declining, or has it retreated from visible consumer brands into upstream chokepoints such as optics, sensors, materials, precision equipment, and components?
- Are Southeast Asia and India truly replacing China, or mainly taking assembly while Chinese/Taiwanese/Japanese/Korean component and engineering ecosystems remain essential?
- How should China respond: stay in assembly, or move up to components, equipment, standards, scenario ecosystems, and domestic platforms?

Potential story frame:

> Apple wants to put the crown of human vision in America, but the neck holding that crown may still be Asian supply chains.

### Humanoid Robots

The humanoid robot topic needs another layer.

Initial direction:

- China may be copying the EV playbook in robotics: low-cost components, fast iteration, dense local supply chains, scenario landing, and export pressure.
- The US may not fear one dancing robot; it may fear China turning machine labor into the next scalable industrial product.

Needs further research before scripting:

- Which components can China localize quickly?
- Which chokepoints remain foreign-dependent?
- Is the market moving from demo videos to real jobs: factories, warehouses, airports, logistics, elderly care, patrol, inspection?
- Which companies become trapped: traditional automation firms, labor-heavy manufacturers, service companies, or Western robot startups with higher costs?

### AI Power Sovereignty

The AI electricity topic should avoid a simple “US weak / China strong” frame.

Better frame:

> AI power war is not only about who has more electricity. It is about who can connect power, land, cooling, chips, models, permits, and capital into one deployable infrastructure machine.

Questions for the US:

- Is the bottleneck generation, grid interconnection, transformers, transmission, permitting, local opposition, power prices, or utility planning cycles?
- What solutions is the US system trying: private power deals, gas, nuclear restarts, SMRs, batteries, demand response, behind-the-meter generation, grid upgrades, hyperscaler financing?

Questions for China:

- Is China’s advantage grid build speed, state coordination, renewables deployment, coal backup, industrial siting, energy bases, or integrated planning?
- Does China have a latecomer advantage by building AI infrastructure around energy planning from the start?

Story rule:

- Do not make it a scoreboard.
- Make it a system competition: two countries using different weapons to solve the same AI infrastructure bottleneck.
