# Deep Research

Use this reference before writing any “观潮” topic where the hidden game depends on supply chains, geopolitics, finance, or platform control.

## Hypothesis Discipline

Start with a testable hypothesis, not a conclusion.

Good:

- “Apple may want to control the human visual entrance, but its XR/glasses hardware may still depend on Asian supply chains.”
- “The AI glasses story may be less about hardware margin and more about repricing real-world advertising inventory.”

Weak:

- “Apple glasses are already fully Americanized.”
- “All manufacturers will follow because Apple released it.”

After browsing, classify the hypothesis:

- `supported`
- `partly supported`
- `not supported`
- `too early / insufficient evidence`

If evidence is mixed, make the contradiction the story.

## Supply-Chain Map

For hardware topics, identify:

- final assembly and manufacturing partners
- key chip suppliers and fabrication region
- display, optical, lens, camera, sensor, battery, and connector suppliers
- operating system, cloud, model, app ecosystem, and data-processing layer
- country/region exposure: China, Taiwan, Japan, South Korea, US, Europe, India, Vietnam, Mexico
- chokepoints: lithography, advanced packaging, micro-OLED, waveguides, battery density, thermal design, AI inference, data compliance

Do not treat “US company brand” as “US supply chain.” Separate platform control from manufacturing dependence.

## Apple / Smart Glasses Angle

For Apple glasses, Vision Pro, Android XR, Meta Ray-Ban, and AI glasses stories, test these angles:

- Platform ambition: who wants to own the visual entrance before the phone screen?
- Supply contradiction: is the product designed by a US platform but assembled or component-dependent in Asia?
- Ecosystem lock: how does the device connect app store, payment, maps, health, ads, identity, and AI agents?
- Geopolitics: how do cameras, maps, face data, enterprise meetings, and public-space sensing become data-sovereignty issues?
- Finance: which assets are repriced: platform stocks, display suppliers, optical suppliers, chips, packaging, batteries, local-commerce ads, enterprise subscriptions?
- Forced followers: which phone, app, search, short-video, local-commerce, and hardware players must follow?

## Story Method

Make the story vivid by assigning roles:

- Platform company: the king trying to move the gate from phone screen to human senses.
- Supplier: the silent hand holding the throat of the hardware dream.
- Capital: the banker pricing control before control is proven.
- Regulator: the guard watching whether public space becomes a private data mine.
- User: the person who gets convenience first, then discovers the default judgment is no longer fully theirs.

Use concrete scenes:

- launch stage
- factory line
- lens closeup
- chip wafer
- city street from first-person view
- meeting room
- store shelf
- stock chart
- map with supply routes

## Writing Standard

Every major section should contain at least one of:

- a conflict
- a reversal
- a concrete scene
- a character motive
- a supply-chain clue
- a financial repricing clue
- a geopolitical risk

Avoid long abstract explanation. If a paragraph sounds like a lecture, rewrite it as a move by a player.
