# Story Craft

Use this before writing any long-form “观潮” script. The goal is not to sound analytical. The goal is to make the audience keep listening because they want to know what happens next.

## Reference Creator Analysis Rule

When studying a living creator or account, extract structure only:

- opening move
- suspense type
- character setup
- conflict escalation
- evidence placement
- reversal timing
- spoken rhythm
- ending device

Do not copy wording, punchlines, paragraph order, private voice, or personal catchphrases. Do not clone or imitate a living person’s exact style. Use the analysis to improve original “观潮” storytelling.

Public reference signals for “听风的蚕”-like storytelling:

- professional concepts are used as props, not as the main character
- the narration feels closer to storytelling / 评书 than to a report
- the viewer is led through “one more door” repeatedly
- major forces are humanized into desire, fear, money, control, face, retreat, and survival
- the story makes the audience feel they are hearing a hidden logic that most people missed

## Story First

Never start from “what I want to explain.” Start from “why would a stranger keep listening?”

Before drafting, write one sentence for each:

- What strange thing happened?
- Why does it feel wrong?
- Who wants something here?
- What is the tool being used?
- What could ordinary people lose or gain?
- What is the next door the audience will want opened?

If these cannot be answered, the topic is not ready for a script.

## Narrative Engine

Use this reusable arc. Do not write it as headings in the final script; use it as the hidden skeleton.

1. **Strange Surface**: Open with a concrete oddity, not a thesis.
2. **Human Desire**: Translate the force behind it into money, control, fear, status, survival, or revenge.
3. **Tool Appears**: Show the device, model, factory, policy, platform, chip, or standard being used as the tool.
4. **First Door Opens**: Reveal why the surface explanation is incomplete.
5. **Evidence Trail**: Place 2-4 verifiable clues like news, suppliers, orders, policy, price, valuation, shipment, or capital movement.
6. **Pressure Ladder**: Each section should raise the stakes: who becomes anxious, who is forced to follow, who pays, who loses choice.
7. **Second Door Opens**: Reveal the hidden consequence: replacement, dependency, toll gate, supply-chain tension, or system competition.
8. **Ordinary Person Hook**: Translate the board back to job, money, consumption, privacy, choice, or opportunity.
9. **Unsettling Takeaway**: End with a defensible sentence that changes how the audience sees the original event.

## Hook Types

Prefer one of these over abstract claims:

- **Odd scene**: “最近的机器人新闻，越来越像一场表演。”
- **Misplaced object**: “这副眼镜看起来戴在人脸上，账本却放在另一张桌子上。”
- **Hidden buyer**: “真正着急的人，不一定是卖产品的人，而是等着收下一道过路费的人。”
- **Role reversal**: “大家都以为它在卖工具，其实它在训练主人。”
- **Money trail**: “先别看发布会，看谁开始提前算钱。”
- **Fear reveal**: “它怕的不是竞争对手追上来，而是自己守住了旧门口，却丢了新门口。”
- **Ordinary trap**: “你先得到方便，后来才发现默认选择已经不是你定的。”

## Spoken Rhythm

Write like a person telling a story, not like a report.

Use:

- varied sentence length
- concrete nouns before abstract nouns
- questions that open the next door
- scene verbs: 走进、拿起、递给、盯着、排队、算账、绕开、卡住、退到
- cautious inference: 像是、更像、可能、一个信号是、连起来看

Avoid:

- mechanical parallelism such as repeated “有，有，有” or “这是，这是，这是”
- slogan stacking
- paragraph after paragraph of short declarative sentences
- abstract nouns without people or objects
- explaining the conclusion before creating curiosity
- ending every section with “这才是重点”

## Anti-Pattern Rewrite Rules

If a draft contains mechanical repetition, rewrite before delivery.

Bad:

> 有电机。有传感器。有供应链。有工厂。有资本。

Better:

> 一台机器人走出实验室以后，最先遇到的不是观众，而是一张工厂采购单。采购经理不会问它像不像人，只会问：关节谁供、坏了谁修、明天能不能多来一百台。

Bad:

> 这是劳动力重新定价。这是制造业重新排队。这是大国竞争。

Better:

> 这时候，机器人就不再是舞台上的节目了。它开始坐到工资表旁边，和人抢同一个格子。

Bad:

> 美国想控制入口，中国想控制供应链，日本想控制上游。

Better:

> 牌桌上没有人只想赢一句口号。有人想守门，有人想造门，有人不站在门口，却握着门轴。

## Retention Audit

Before finalizing, mark the script every 30-45 seconds. Each segment must contain at least one:

- unanswered question
- new clue
- human desire
- concrete scene
- reversal
- money trail
- danger or loss
- ordinary-person implication

Reject a segment if it only explains.

## Drafting Workflow

1. Research facts and save source links.
2. Extract the human desires behind the facts.
3. Assign dynamic story roles.
4. Write the first 60 seconds only.
5. Run the retention audit.
6. Only then expand into the full script.

If the first 60 seconds do not work as a story, do not continue expanding.
