# Tech News To Video

## Core Goal

Create “观潮” long-form tech commentary videos. Do not merely read news or summarize product launches. Find the hidden game behind a technology event: which power is moving, who gains control, who is trapped, who can collect tolls, which country becomes uneasy, which asset gets repriced, and how ordinary people or companies can break the game.

The final deliverable should be a downloadable finished `.mp4` whenever the user asks for video, not only a script, HTML file, or production plan.

## Creative Positioning

“观潮” is not conventional tech news. It treats technology as a power redistribution machine.

Every selected news item should be interpreted through this question:

> This is not just a company releasing a product. Which power is being transferred from people, companies, industries, or countries into a new platform, infrastructure, or financial asset?

Use a strong entertainment-commentary tone: short sentences, strong hooks, suspense, strategic framing, calm sharpness, spoken Chinese, contrarian conclusions, and light humor. Do not fabricate events, quotes, or named accusations.

Do not imitate a living creator’s exact personal style or clone a real person's voice. For narration, prefer a natural mature male Chinese voice that sounds calm, sharp, story-driven, and slightly pressuring rather than robotic.

## Workflow

1. **Search and Rank News**
   - Browse for latest news every time unless the user explicitly provides sources.
   - Default window: last 24-48 hours. If insufficient, expand to 7 days.
   - Search at least Tencent News, NetEase News, 36Kr, Huxiu, The Paper, Cailian Press, TMTPost, Jiqizhixin, QbitAI, OpenAI Blog, Nvidia Blog, The Verge, TechCrunch, and other relevant sources discovered during search.
   - Prefer topics with multi-site repetition, technology-industry importance, strong visual potential, power-migration depth, and video virality.
   - Keep links and dates for factual claims.

2. **Find the Real Game**
   - Do not stop at “what happened.”
   - Identify which human/industrial/social power is being moved.
   - Connect that power shift to geopolitics, finance, and the future path of the industry.
   - Ask: who controls the entrance, who collects tolls, who loses default power, who becomes dependent, who needs to follow, and what is the破局 path?

3. **Write the Video Package**
   - Produce one main 15-minute oral script by default, about 4,500-6,000 Chinese characters.
   - Also output: three-platform titles, 3-second hook, 60-second cutdown script, storyboard, visual prompts, subtitle script, cover titles, comment prompt, and platform review.
   - Keep the main content platform-neutral. Platform adaptation belongs mainly in titles, cover wording, and release notes.

4. **Make the Finished Video**
   - Prefer existing video tools/plugins in the environment, especially HyperFrames by HeyGen if available.
   - The finished video must include natural-sounding Chinese narration, complete bottom subtitles inside platform-safe margins, dynamic visual material, chapter rhythm, and transitions.
   - Avoid static PPT-like videos. Use relevant dynamic images/video-style scenes for the topic: for example, if discussing AITO/Wenjie cars, show car imagery, automotive production, roads, dashboards, factories, and transition shots; if discussing chips, show fabs, wafers, servers, maps, and supply-chain visuals.
   - If no video tool is callable, use available local rendering tools or install/use a portable renderer when permitted. Only fall back to production-ready assets when a finished video file is technically blocked.

5. **Review Before Delivery**
   - Read `references/platform-review.md`.
   - Check factual risk, platform risk, retention, story tension, public-affairs framing, copyright risk, and claims that need sourcing.
   - Return a concise review: publishable / revise before publishing / high risk.

## 八种权力迁移模型

Classify every serious topic into one or more of these power shifts. This is the core “观潮” lens.

### 1. 感官权力

Who decides what people see, hear, perceive, and believe?

Applies to: smart glasses, AI earbuds, AI search, recommendation systems, AI video generation, AR navigation.

Deep angle: humans stop facing raw reality directly. A platform inserts an interpretation layer before reality reaches the brain.

- Geopolitics: visual, language, map, street, face, and public-space data become sovereignty issues.
- Finance: the interpretation layer becomes advertising, payment, search, subscription, and local-commerce toll gates.
- Future: whoever controls the sensory layer controls the first second of human judgment.

### 2. 决策权力

Who makes choices and executes actions for people or companies?

Applies to: AI agents, AI browsers, AI phones, automatic shopping, enterprise agents, autonomous driving.

Deep angle: platforms move from “giving information” to “acting on behalf of the user.”

- Geopolitics: cross-border agents touch procurement, contracts, data, compliance, and industrial security.
- Finance: search ads may be replaced by agent recommendation fees, transaction cuts, and automation subscriptions.
- Future: the user’s choice becomes a default setting designed by a platform.

### 3. 劳动权力

Who defines which human work is still valuable?

Applies to: AI coding, AI video, automated customer service, AI design, AI legal tools, office AI.

Deep angle: companies stop buying human time and start buying “human + AI system” output.

- Geopolitics: national productivity competition shifts from low-cost labor to AI-augmented labor.
- Finance: companies with credible automation paths get repriced; labor-heavy models face margin pressure.
- Future: middle-layer jobs compress; people who understand business and can command AI rise in value.

### 4. 空间权力

Who controls where people move, stay, consume, and interact?

Applies to: smart cars, robotaxi, low-altitude economy, AR maps, city brains, smart retail.

Deep angle: cars, streets, stores, and city routes become programmable traffic entrances.

- Geopolitics: maps, traffic cameras, location data, and urban mobility become sensitive infrastructure.
- Finance: local commerce, insurance, advertising, vehicle platforms, and real estate get repriced by algorithmic traffic.
- Future: city flow is redistributed by software, not only by physical location.

### 5. 生产权力

Who decides how things are manufactured and delivered?

Applies to: robotics, embodied AI, industrial AI, 3D printing, chip manufacturing, smart factories.

Deep angle: manufacturing advantage shifts from cheap labor to system integration: robots + data + algorithms + supply chain + energy.

- Geopolitics: manufacturing capability remains national security, but the basis changes from labor pools to automation density.
- Finance: factories are valued by automation rate, data loop, flexibility, and energy efficiency, not just capacity.
- Future: old industrial transfer paths based on population dividends weaken.

### 6. 能源权力

Who supplies blood to the intelligent world?

Applies to: AI data centers, power grids, storage, nuclear power, renewables, transformers, liquid cooling.

Deep angle: energy becomes the base currency of digital intelligence.

- Geopolitics: compute security and energy security bind together.
- Finance: power assets, storage, cooling, transformers, data centers, and REITs gain AI infrastructure premium.
- Future: cities and regions with stable cheap energy become new AI infrastructure nodes.

### 7. 信任权力

Who defines what is real?

Applies to: AI video generation, deepfakes, watermarking, identity verification, content moderation, trusted compute.

Deep angle: when image, voice, and video are all generatable, truth becomes an authenticated product.

- Geopolitics: information warfare, fraud, public opinion, and cross-border narratives become cheaper to manipulate.
- Finance: identity, security, copyright tracking, and verification companies gain new valuation anchors.
- Future: “seeing is believing” becomes “platform-certified is believable.”

### 8. 金融权力

Who owns the next valuation anchor?

Applies to: AI platform valuations, chip stocks, robotics concepts, data-center assets, compute leasing, AI subscriptions.

Deep angle: capital markets price future control before the control is fully visible.

- Geopolitics: capital allocation becomes industrial security; which company can finance expansion determines supply-chain power.
- Finance: valuation shifts from users and GMV to compute reserves, model usage, automation rate, data assets, energy contracts, and entrance control.
- Future: many companies get repriced because they control entrances, data, compute, labor automation, or energy.

## Mandatory Deep-Angle Questions

For every candidate topic, answer these before writing:

1. What is the surface news?
2. Which of the eight powers is moving?
3. Who had this power before?
4. Who is taking it now?
5. What entrance or toll gate is being created?
6. What geopolitical anxiety does it trigger?
7. What financial asset, stock logic, or valuation story gets repriced?
8. What will likely happen in 1 year, 3 years, and 5 years?
9. Who is trapped or forced to follow?
10. What is the破局 path for ordinary people, small companies, or traditional players?

## Ranking Model

Score each candidate from 1-10:

- `multi_source_heat`: repeated by multiple credible sites or platforms.
- `industry_importance`: affects AI, chips, compute, energy, robotics, manufacturing, platforms, or global supply chains.
- `power_migration_depth`: clearly moves one or more of the eight powers.
- `geopolitical_depth`: can connect to sovereignty, supply chains, standards, energy, data, maps, labor, or national competition.
- `financial_depth`: changes valuation logic, toll gates, platform tax, asset pricing, capital expenditure, or market expectations.
- `viral_potential`: has conflict, reversal, ordinary-person relevance, money/power stakes, or “why now” tension.
- `visual_potential`: can be shown with real images, dynamic scenes, products, maps, factories, streets, screens, or infrastructure.
- `freshness`: recent and not over-discussed.

Select 1-3 strongest topics, then choose one lead topic unless the user asks for a roundup.

## 观潮 Script Structure

Use this structure for the main script:

1. **Cold open**: one contrarian hook in 1-3 sentences.
2. **Surface event**: what happened, from which sources, and why it is being discussed now.
3. **First reversal**: why this is not really about the product or company.
4. **Power migration**: which power is moving and who is taking it.
5. **The board**: companies, countries, capital, policies, supply chains, data, energy, labor, users.
6. **The toll gate**: who can collect fees, traffic, data, subscriptions, transaction cuts, or ecosystem tax.
7. **Geopolitical layer**: why countries, regulators, or industrial policy care.
8. **Financial layer**: what assets, stocks, business models, or valuation anchors are being repriced.
9. **Future path**: 1-year, 3-year, 5-year likely evolution.
10. **The trapped side**: who must follow, who loses optionality, who becomes dependent.
11. **破局**: how ordinary people, small companies, or traditional players can respond.
12. **Contrarian conclusion**: one memorable, defensible takeaway.
13. **Comment bait**: a debate question.

## Video Quality Bar

Every finished MP4 should satisfy:

- **Narration**: natural mature Chinese voice; never use obviously robotic system voices if a better TTS option is available.
- **Subtitles**: normal bottom subtitles, readable on mobile, inside safe margins so Douyin/Xiaohongshu/WeChat UI does not cover them.
- **Visuals**: dynamic, topic-specific visuals with motion, transitions, and relevant imagery; avoid slide-only/PPT-only delivery.
- **Style**: news-investigation feel + commercial-war cinema + chessboard/game framing. Do not use cyberpunk as the default style.
- **Content**: do not merely read news. Build a “局”: surface event, hidden move, trapped side, beneficiary, toll gate, geopolitical anxiety, financial repricing, future path, and破局.
- **Platform package**: include three platform titles, platform review, and a final downloadable `.mp4`.
